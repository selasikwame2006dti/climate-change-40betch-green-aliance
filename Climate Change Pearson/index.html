Frequently Asked Questions

What is climate change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas, which produces heat-trapping gases.

How does climate change affect Ghana?

In Ghana, climate change manifests through changes in rainfall patterns, increased temperatures, and more frequent extreme weather events like floods and droughts. These impacts severely affect agriculture, water resources, and coastal communities.

What is Galamsey and its link to climate change?

Galamsey refers to illegal small-scale mining. It contributes to climate change through deforestation (clearing forests for mining), which reduces carbon sequestration. It also pollutes water bodies and agricultural lands, destroying ecosystems and livelihoods.

How can young people in Ghana get involved?

Young people can get involved by educating themselves, reducing their carbon footprint, advocating for sustainable policies, participating in local environmental initiatives, and joining community clean-up efforts. This website provides resources and ideas to help you start.
